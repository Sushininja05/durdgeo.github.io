1. Progress since DEMO!!


I usually do a catch-up blog entry after every year, so treat this one like that, yeah?
Grandman 2 (or Grandman's Unbearable Existence) has been trucking along, ever since the editor I've:
  • -Completely revamped the enemy AI to allow for throwing them and to prevent lag.
  • -Overhauled a ton of objects, menus, and Grandman himself to be more responsive.
  • -Made a world editor and several other systems to support custom levels/worlds fully.
  • -Made a proper title-screen (although WIP!!) and proper options menu (WIP too).
  • -Fixed a sea of glitches and other things, while adding new things that fixing the glitches allowed me to add.
  • -Various tech and new interactions for Grandman, I won't spoil them though!

So, all in all, how many objects ARE there?
I'll let you be the judge:


Not only that, but there's B1/B2-variants for the majority of the objects, allowing them to behave differently. If that wasn't enough for your level-making needs (mine actually since I have to make the story), there's 6 weather effects to choose from, adjustable gravity and double-jumps, customizable world-maps, and fully replacable graphics for the levels.

I think I've cornered the market for level-editor shenanigans, outside of, y'kno, just making your game or romhacking SMW.

2. Then what's the hold-up? It's done right?

I've been doin' a lot!

Then, I'll have to make the bosses and cutscenes, while ironing stuff out in playtesting sessions and whatnot. So, we'll be there soon, I promise-- especially with school easing up for me. Just don't get yer hopes up, kapeesh?
